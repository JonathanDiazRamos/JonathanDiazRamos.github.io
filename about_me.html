Navigating Graduate School

My journey into graduate school and research was driven by a deep curiosity about how technology, specifically artificial intelligence, could address societal inequalities. As a GEM Fellow, I explored how AI could help bridge gaps in understanding community needs, particularly the lack of real-time, accurate data about socioeconomic conditions. For my Master’s thesis, I developed a cost-effective approach using Google Street View images to identify environmental indicators—such as garbage, damaged roads, and limited green spaces—that correlate with socioeconomic status. By applying computer vision techniques like object detection and image segmentation, I was able to validate these markers with machine learning classifiers, showing how they could differentiate between disadvantaged and non-disadvantaged communities. This research suggested a scalable solution for assessing community needs across the U.S., using widely available data to inform policy and resource allocation.

Alongside my technical research, I have been deeply involved in advocating for first-generation students, leading initiatives to address the unique challenges we face in higher education. These experiences have reinforced my commitment to leveraging AI for social good, particularly in creating accessible, actionable insights to improve the lives of underserved populations. Ultimately, I hope to continue my work on developing machine learning-driven solutions in areas such as sustainable energy, environmental monitoring, and community health—areas where technology can drive meaningful, measurable impact for marginalized communities.
